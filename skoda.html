Core Problem

The previous app was a direct port of the web portal — a dense feature list rather than a contextual, mobile-first experience. Connected features were buried behind menus, and the interface felt generic rather than premium. Drivers couldn't easily find or trust the app to manage their vehicle.

🎯
Design Goals
  • Surface the top 5 actions within 2 taps from home
  • Build a visual vehicle status dashboard that feels alive
  • Design for multi-market localisation from day one
  • Reduce support tickets related to feature discoverability by 40%
🌍
Who We Designed For

Škoda owners across 30+ European markets — ranging from young urban commuters using the app daily to check range and pre-condition their car, to occasional users who primarily needed emergency assistance and service reminders.

Context & Pain Points

Driving insights from diary studies and in-car observation

Usage data told us features were underused, but not why. We needed to understand the real moments drivers wanted to interact with their car remotely — and what prevented them from doing so.

We ran 2-week driver diary studies with 16 participants across Germany, Czech Republic, and the UK, followed by 12 in-car contextual sessions and a teardown of 5 competitor OEM apps.

Users' Talk

Drivers told us exactly what they needed

The diary studies surfaced a clear pattern: drivers wanted fast access to a handful of high-frequency actions — lock, climate control, range, and locate. Everything else could be secondary.

The existing app made even these simple tasks feel like an expedition. Quotes from participants became our north star for the redesign.

Improvements

Prioritising what matters most to drivers

After the initial diary phase with daily commuters, we ran a second round with occasional users — revealing that the discoverability problem affected both power users and casual ones equally, just at different points in the journey.

We mapped every identified issue to a driver need and assigned severity based on frequency of mention, impact on daily use, and estimated engineering effort.

The Core Insight

Connected car apps had the potential to deepen the relationship between owner and vehicle — but most felt like afterthoughts bolted onto hardware. The bar for quality was low. We set out to raise it.

V1.0 AS IS Critical Issues What was broken
✗
Remote control features were 4–6 taps deep from the home screen — 31% of users gave up trying to use climate control remotely within the first month.
Confirmed by app analytics: 68% of climate control sessions abandoned before action was triggered.
✗
The home screen showed a static list of 20+ features with no hierarchy or context — creating a visual wall that overwhelmed both first-time and returning users.
✗
The app had no memory of recent actions, forcing users to navigate the full feature tree every time — even for daily-use actions like range check or lock confirmation.
