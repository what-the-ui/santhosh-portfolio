Overview

Redesigning the way millions buy and sell fashion

Poshmark is one of the largest social commerce platforms in the US, with millions of buyers and sellers transacting daily. As the design lead, I spearheaded a comprehensive redesign of the iOS and Android apps to modernise the experience, improve discoverability, and reduce friction across the core buying and selling flows.

Core Problem

Poshmark's app had grown feature-by-feature over 8 years without a cohesive design system, creating a fragmented experience that felt dated and confusing to a new generation of resellers.

Design Goals
  • Reduce listing time from ~4 min to under 90 seconds
  • Consolidate 3 navigation patterns into one clear IA
  • Build a unified design system with reusable components
  • Increase seller onboarding completion rate by 25%

Who We Designed For

Primarily Gen-Z and Millennial resellers who expected a TikTok-level intuitive experience — and buyers who craved better product discovery without the noise of an overcrowded feed.

The Problem

The existing app had grown organically over years, resulting in inconsistent UI patterns, a cluttered home feed, and a listing flow that frustrated new sellers — leading to high drop-off rates.

Improvements

Defining what to fix first

After research with new sellers, we ran a second round with experienced power sellers to stress-test our findings and validate edge-case scenarios.

We synthesised findings into a prioritised improvement report and presented it to product leadership. Every design decision that followed was anchored to a specific research insight.

Research Report ↗

We categorised issues into three severity levels:
Minor Small inconsistencies and non-critical UX friction
Moderate Noticeable friction impacting task completion rates
Critical Flow-breaking issues causing user abandonment

Issues

V1.0 Critical
  • The listing flow had 12 separate steps — causing 62% of new sellers to abandon before completing their first listing.
    Highest drop-off at step 4 (photo requirements) and step 9 (category tree depth).
  • Home feed showed undifferentiated content regardless of browsing history — making discovery feel random and reducing return visit frequency by 38%.
  • Three coexisting navigation patterns (tab bar, hamburger menu, swipe gestures) created inconsistent mental models across the app.

The Solution

V2.0 TO BE
  • Redesigned the listing flow from 12 steps to 5 — grouping photos, title, and category into a single smart screen with AI-assisted tagging. Listing time dropped from 4 min to under 90 seconds.
  • Rebuilt the home feed with a personalised discovery engine — surfacing liked categories, followed sellers, and trending styles based on real browsing behaviour.
  • Consolidated all navigation into a single bottom tab bar with consistent gesture rules — eliminating conflicting patterns and reducing navigation-related support tickets by 40%.
