Overview

Designing an AI-first platform for IT professionals who hate complexity

SuperOps is a unified PSA + RMM platform built for Managed Service Providers. As lead product designer, I shaped the end-to-end experience — from core workflows like ticketing and asset management to AI-powered features that automate repetitive tasks. The challenge: make enterprise-grade power feel effortlessly intuitive.

Core Problem

The MSP software market was fragmented — technicians toggled between legacy tools that hadn't evolved in a decade. SuperOps had to earn trust by being genuinely better, not just prettier. Every design decision had to reduce cognitive load for engineers already stretched thin.

🎯
Design Goals
  • Unify PSA + RMM into a single seamless workflow
  • Reduce time-to-complete for core tasks by 40%
  • Build AI features that feel like a natural assistant, not a gimmick
  • Achieve a CSAT score above 4.5 within 6 months of launch
🧑‍💻
Who We Designed For

MSP owners running lean teams of 5–50 technicians, and the technicians themselves — dealing with hundreds of client endpoints, service tickets, and alerts daily. Power users who needed speed above all else.

Context & Pain Points

How IT professionals actually work — and where software fails them

Although usage analytics gave us funnel data, qualitative research was critical to understand the deeply contextual, interruption-heavy nature of MSP work — and why legacy tools amplified rather than reduced cognitive overload.

We ran 25 interviews and 4 on-site shadowing sessions across different MSP scales, from solo operators to 50-person teams. Each session averaged 90 minutes.

Improvements

From research to design decisions

After round one of discovery with technicians, we ran a validation round with MSP owners and business leads — who added a strategic dimension to the pain: every minute lost to software friction was billable time lost.

We highlighted every issue, then ranked them by frequency, severity, and business impact. The prioritised list became our design backlog for the first 6 sprints.

The Core Insight

Legacy PSA tools were built for the process, not the person. Technicians spent more time navigating software than solving client problems — and the market had accepted this as normal.

V1.0 AS IS Critical Issues What was broken
✗
The platform had 11+ separate tool contexts — technicians lost critical information when switching between tickets, assets, and monitoring, leading to 38% re-open rates on tickets.
Confirmed through task analysis: average of 6 context switches per ticket resolution.
✗
The dashboard showed static data snapshots with no contextual actions — technicians had to navigate 4+ screens to action any alert they saw.
✗
AI features were buried in a separate "Labs" section, used by fewer than 12% of active users despite being the platform's core differentiator.
